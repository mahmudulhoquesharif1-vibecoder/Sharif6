Our Story

A Legacy of Precision

Founded in the heart of Geneva in 1887, Horlogerie has dedicated over a century to the art of fine watchmaking. Every timepiece that leaves our atelier carries the spirit of generations of master craftsmen.

We believe that a watch is not merely an instrument of time — it is a companion, a statement, and an heirloom. Each movement is assembled by hand, each case finished to mirror perfection.

Est. 1887 — Geneva, Switzerland

Crafting Time
Since 1887

The house of Horlogerie was born in the workshops of the Rue de la Corraterie in Geneva. Three generations of the Beaumont family have guided its philosophy: that a watch must transcend its function and become an object of desire and meaning.

"To build a watch is to capture a moment of perfection that will last a century."
— ÉDOUARD BEAUMONT, FOUNDER
Our Journey

A History of
Excellence

1887
Édouard Beaumont establishes the atelier at Rue de la Corraterie, Geneva. First watches produced using traditional lever escapements.
1924
The Éternité collection debuts, featuring the now-iconic midnight blue dial. Worn by royalty across Europe.
1967
Third generation Pierre Beaumont introduces the skeletonized movement, winning the Grand Prix at the Geneva Watchmaking competition.
2001
Launch of the Chronos Sport collection. Silicon escapement technology reduces friction by 40%, setting a new standard.
Today
86 master craftsmen. Every watch still assembled by hand. 137 years of unbroken tradition, carried into the future.
